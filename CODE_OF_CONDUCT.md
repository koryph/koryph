# Code of Conduct

This project has one social rule, and it is short: **be kind, and argue
about the work.**

## The standard

- **Kindness is the default.** Write to people the way you'd want to be
  written to on your worst day. Assume good faith; most friction is a
  misreading, not an attack.
- **Critique code, never people.** "This function re-reads the file on
  every tick" is a contribution. "How could you write this" is not.
- **Merit decides.** Ideas win on evidence, correctness, and craft — not on
  who proposed them, how long they've been here, or how loudly they argue.
  A first-time contributor's good patch beats a maintainer's bad one.
- **Skill is not a license.** Being brilliant, prolific, or right does not
  buy anyone an exemption from the first rule. A contributor who is
  technically excellent but persistently demeaning, hostile, or
  contemptuous toward others makes the project worse, and will be treated
  accordingly — no matter how good the code is.

## Unacceptable behavior

Personal attacks, insults, and name-calling; sustained hostility or
contempt toward another participant; harassment of any person, in project
spaces or directed at them because of their participation here; and
bad-faith participation (trolling, deliberate derailment, dogpiling).

## Scope

This applies in every project space — issues, pull requests, code review,
discussions, commit messages — and to conduct outside them when it targets
a participant for their involvement in the project.

sustained or severe behavior. Maintainers hold themselves to this document
first, and maintainer decisions on conduct are final.
